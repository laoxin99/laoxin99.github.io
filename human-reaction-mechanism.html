Book Overview / Theory Preview

Human Reaction Mechanism

A Structural Model of Individual Behavior. This page gives a public overview of the theory without hosting the contracted English manuscript or the full book text.

Core Question

Before behavior appears, what structure has already made it possible?

Human Reaction Mechanism is an original, non-normative theoretical framework. It does not begin by judging whether a person is good, bad, healthy, weak, or strong. It asks a lower-level structural question: under what conditions can a living system generate a response, pass through internal adjudication, and produce behavior?

The theory treats operability, stability maintenance, stimulus input, imbalance, emotional marking, behavioral adjudication, freezing, consciousness, thought, subjectivity, evaluation, and experience registration as connected layers in a reaction-level model of individual behavior.

Positioning

Not a psychology textbook, not a moral system.

The book is positioned between psychology, behavioral science, philosophy of action, systems theory, and AI modeling. It is not a clinical manual and does not replace psychological counseling, psychotherapy, psychiatry, or neuroscience.

Its main contribution is a structural language for describing how behavior becomes possible before it is later explained as intention, emotion, value, personality, or conscious choice.

Four Safe Public Theses

Emotion marks imbalance.

Emotion is treated as a structural marker of imbalance or rebalancing, not as the direct ruler of behavior.

Behavior requires adjudication.

Behavior is not reduced to stimulus-response. It passes through a behavior-leading adjudication structure.

Consciousness has a boundary.

Consciousness can intervene and assist under conflict, but it is not described as the highest behavioral judge.

Subjectivity is constrained difference.

The person is neither a passive machine nor an unlimited sovereign subject, but a system capable of active difference under constraints.

Book Structure

Five-part architecture.

The public structure below is based on the Chinese final manuscript outline. It is shared as a high-level map, not as a release of the manuscript.

  1. Life system and stability maintenance: operability, life stability, organ stability, and psychological stability.
  2. Stimulus, imbalance, and emotional marking: how inputs become imbalance and why emotion appears as a marker.
  3. Behavioral adjudication, freezing, and path solidification: why behavior needs adjudication and how repeated paths become patterns.
  4. Consciousness, thought, and subjectivity interface: when consciousness intervenes, where thought arises, and how active difference is positioned.
  5. Evaluation, experience registration, and reaction loop: how outcomes are marked, stored, and folded back into future reactions.
