Human Reaction Mechanism

A theoretical framework for describing how human beings respond to internal and external stimuli through reaction, adjudication, emotional movement, cognition, behavior, and long-term pattern formation.

Core Idea

Reaction is treated as a foundational unit.

Human Reaction Mechanism begins from a simple but deep question: before a person explains, chooses, or acts, what reaction process has already been formed inside the body, mind, emotion, memory, and situation?

The theory attempts to describe shared reaction-level structures behind behavior, cognition, emotion, suffering, adaptation, and intelligent action. It is not a replacement for existing psychology, but a proposed lower-level framework through which multiple psychological themes may be re-examined.

Key Themes

What the theory studies.

  • How external and internal stimuli become reactions.
  • How pleasure, suffering, tension, relief, and stability shape behavior.
  • How repeated reactions become tendencies, habits, and long-range patterns.
  • How cognition and emotion interact before conscious explanation appears.
  • How an intelligent system might model reaction, boundary, persistence, and adjudication.
Relationship To ZhiNao

From theory to mechanism-based intelligence.

ZhiNao is an engineering research line inspired by Human Reaction Mechanism. It explores whether intelligence can be understood not only as generation, but also as reaction, internal adjudication, boundary control, persistence, and long-range behavioral guidance.

CognitiveBridge-v0 is one small public demo in this direction. It shows how an external thought stream can be constrained into low-permission candidates rather than directly controlling actions or writing into the main system state.

Public Boundary

What this theory page does not claim.

This page is a public theory overview. It does not provide medical advice, psychotherapy, diagnosis, treatment, crisis intervention, or clinical guidance.

It also does not claim that Human Reaction Mechanism has unified all psychology, that AGI has been achieved, or that ZhiNao has been publicly released as a full intelligent system.
